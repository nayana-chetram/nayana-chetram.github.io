About Me

I’m a Mathematics and Information Systems student at Hofstra University and a member of the Rabinowitz Honors College, with a minor in Scientific Reasoning & Data Science.

My interests sit at the intersection of artificial intelligence, data science, and quantitative analysis. I’m drawn to identifying patterns in data, building structured frameworks, and translating complex information into clear insights.

Through my coursework and technical training, I’ve built a strong foundation in data mining, business analytics, probability, statistics, and programming. I am also an AWS Certified Solutions Architect – Associate, which has strengthened my understanding of cloud architecture, scalable systems, and deploying data-driven solutions.

Leadership and Focus

To further develop this focus, I founded Hofstra AI Society, the university’s first student-led organization centered on analytics, artificial intelligence, and emerging technologies. The organization is focused on making AI more accessible through technical workshops, hands-on projects, and conversations around responsible use of data.

That same analytical focus has carried into competitive environments. In Spring 2025, I placed first in Hofstra’s Cybersecurity Case Competition, applying structured analysis and systems-based thinking in a team-driven setting.

I’m focused on continuing to build at the intersection of data, systems, and structured analysis.
