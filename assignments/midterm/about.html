About Me

I’m a Mathematical Finance major at Hofstra University and a member of the Rabinowitz Honors College, triple-minoring in Information Systems & Business Analytics, Accounting, and Economics.

My interests sit at the intersection of artificial intelligence, data science, and quantitative finance. I’m drawn to identifying patterns in data, building structured frameworks, and translating complex information into clear insights.

Through my coursework and technical training, I’ve built a strong foundation in statistical analysis and financial modeling, with a growing focus on predictive modeling, artificial intelligence, and machine learning algorithms. I am also an AWS Certified Solutions Architect Associate, which has strengthened my understanding of cloud architecture, scalable systems, and deploying data-driven solutions.

To further develop this focus, I founded Hofstra AI Society, the university’s first student-run organization dedicated to artificial intelligence. The organization is focused on making AI more accessible to students through technical workshops, real-world projects, and conversations around emerging technologies. My goal is to build a space where students can not only learn concepts, but actually apply them in meaningful ways and collaborate across disciplines.

That same analytical focus has carried into competitive environments. In Spring 2025, I placed first in Hofstra’s Cybersecurity Case Study Competition, applying structured analysis and systems-based thinking in a team-driven setting.

I’m focused on continuing to build at the intersection of data, systems, and structured analysis.
